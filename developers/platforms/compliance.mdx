## Merchant onboarding requirements

Your platform must collect and verify the following before provisioning a merchant:

### Individual merchants

- Full legal name
- Email address (verified)
- Country of residence
- Date of birth (for age verification)
- Government-issued ID (for `ENHANCED` tier+)

### Business merchants

- Legal entity name
- Business registration number
- Country of incorporation
- Beneficial owner information (25%+ ownership)
- Business address
- Business description and website
- Government-issued ID of authorized representative (for `ENHANCED` tier+)

<Note>
  Pandabase performs additional screening on all merchants, but your platform is
  responsible for the initial collection and verification of this information.
</Note>

## Transaction monitoring

Platforms must implement real-time transaction monitoring for:

- **Unusual volume spikes**: Merchants exceeding 3x their average daily volume
- **High-value transactions**: Transactions above the merchant's typical average
- **Rapid velocity**: Multiple transactions from the same customer in a short window
- **Geographic anomalies**: Transactions from unexpected countries
- **Refund patterns**: Merchants with refund rates exceeding 10%

### Reporting obligations

When suspicious activity is detected:

1. Flag the merchant in your system
2. Notify Pandabase via `POST /v2/platforms/merchants/{merchantId}/flag`
3. Provide supporting evidence within 48 hours
4. Cooperate with any subsequent investigation

```bash
POST /v2/platforms/merchants/{merchantId}/flag
Authorization: Platform plt_xxx
X-Platform-Signature: {signature}

{
  "reason": "SUSPICIOUS_ACTIVITY",
  "description": "Merchant volume increased 10x in 24 hours with high refund rate",
  "evidence": {
    "dailyVolume": 50000,
    "averageDailyVolume": 5000,
    "refundRate": 0.15,
    "flaggedTransactions": ["pti_xxx", "pti_yyy"]
  }
}
```

## PCI compliance

| Integration type | PCI requirement |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Hosted payment page (redirect) | SAQ-A |
| Pandabase.js (iframe embed) | SAQ-A |
| Custom payment form (Pandabase.js Elements) | SAQ-A-EP |
| Direct API (raw card data) | Not supported |

<Warning>
  Pandabase does not support direct card data handling via API. All card data
  must flow through Pandabase.js or the hosted payment page. Platforms found
  handling raw card data will be immediately terminated.
</Warning>

## Dispute management

Platforms are responsible for ensuring merchants respond to disputes within the required SLA:

| Dispute type | Evidence deadline | Platform responsibility |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Fraud | 7 days | Facilitate merchant evidence submission |
| Product not received | 7 days | Provide delivery/fulfillment proof |
| Not as described | 7 days | Provide product documentation |
| Duplicate | 7 days | Provide transaction records |

If a merchant fails to submit evidence, Pandabase submits available MoR data automatically, but the dispute is more likely to be lost.

### Dispute rate monitoring

| Dispute rate | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| < 0.5% | No action |
| 0.5% – 0.75% | Platform warning |
| 0.75% – 1.0% | Merchant restricted, platform notified |
| > 1.0% | Merchant suspended, platform review triggered |

## Data retention

Platforms must retain the following for **7 years**:

- Merchant onboarding records (KYB documentation)
- Transaction records and metadata
- Dispute evidence and correspondence
- Refund records and reasons
- Platform fee records
- Compliance flags and investigation records

## Annual review

All Platform Partners undergo an annual compliance review:

1. **Self-assessment questionnaire** — Sent in Q4 each year
2. **Documentation review** — Pandabase reviews your KYB procedures, monitoring systems, and dispute handling
3. **Volume review** — Analysis of transaction patterns, dispute rates, and refund rates across your merchant base
4. **Certification** — Upon passing, your platform is re-certified for another year

Platforms that fail the annual review enter a **90-day remediation period**. Failure to remediate results in platform suspension.

## Prohibited activities

Platforms must not onboard merchants engaged in:

- Illegal goods or services
- Counterfeit or IP-infringing products
- Unregulated financial services
- Gambling (without proper licensing)
- Adult content (without age verification)
- Pyramid or multi-level marketing schemes
- Cryptocurrency exchange (without proper licensing)
- Weapons, ammunition, or explosives
- Controlled substances

<Warning>
  If your platform is found onboarding merchants engaged in prohibited
  activities, your platform account will be suspended immediately and pending
  settlements will be held pending investigation.
</Warning>
